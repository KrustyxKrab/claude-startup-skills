# Question Bank

Structured questions for Phase 1 of `startup-research`. Claude adapts these based on what the founder has already shared — don't ask what they've answered, don't use their exact phrasing back at them.

---

## Tier 1: Essential (always ask, in this order)

**Q1 — Idea description**
> "Describe your idea in 2-3 sentences. What does it do and who is it for?"

*Purpose:* Seed for all research. Extract: product category, target customer, core action.
*Red flags:* If they can't describe it clearly in 3 sentences, the idea is likely not yet formed enough to research well. Help them sharpen before proceeding.

---

**Q2 — Problem articulation**
> "What problem does this solve? How do you know this problem exists?"

*Purpose:* Separates problem-aware founders from solution-first founders.
*What good looks like:* "I noticed X when working at Y, and talked to 5 people who all said Z."
*Red flags:* "I just think people would want this." → Flag for Stage 1 validation. Note in briefing that problem evidence is thin.

---

**Q3 — Current alternatives**
> "How do people currently deal with this problem today?"

*Purpose:* Reveals competitor landscape, problem severity, and switching cost.
*What good looks like:* Names specific tools, workarounds, or manual processes.
*Red flag:* "They don't" or "nothing exists" → almost always false. Either the problem isn't painful enough to solve, or the founder hasn't looked.

---

**Q4 — Founder-market fit**
> "Why you? What unique insight, experience, or advantage do you bring to this?"

*Purpose:* Assesses founder-market fit and unfair advantages.
*What good looks like:* Domain expertise, proprietary distribution, unique technology, lived experience with the problem.
*Note:* Don't challenge this directly — note gaps in the briefing for the validator to probe.

---

**Q5 — First customer**
> "Who is your ideal first customer? Be as specific as possible — job title, company size, behavior, context."

*Purpose:* Forces segmentation. "Everyone" is nobody.
*What good looks like:* "Marketing managers at B2B SaaS companies with 50-200 employees who currently use 3+ tools for campaign tracking."
*If they say "everyone":* Ask follow-up: "If you could only sell to 10 customers to start, who would they be and why?"

---

## Tier 2: Context-Dependent (ask if not yet clear)

**Q6 — Business model**
> "Is this B2B or B2C? Or both?"

*Why it matters:* Fundamentally changes research direction, validation approach, and everything downstream.
*If unclear:* "Who writes the check — an individual spending personal money, or someone at a company spending a business budget?"

---

**Q7 — Geography**
> "What geography or market are you targeting first?"

*Why it matters:* Affects market sizing, regulatory research, competitive scan.
*Default assumption if not asked:* US, unless context suggests otherwise.

---

**Q8 — Existing evidence**
> "Do you have any evidence of demand already — conversations with potential customers, a waitlist, letters of intent, an early prototype people have used?"

*Why it matters:* Calibrates how much Stage 1 work is needed.
*If yes:* Ask to share the specific evidence so it can be included in the briefing.

---

**Q9 — Revenue model**
> "What's the rough revenue model you're imagining? Subscription, marketplace take-rate, one-time purchase, usage-based, advertising, something else?"

*Why it matters:* Shapes business model research in Dimension 7.

---

**Q10 — "Why now"**
> "Is there a specific technology, regulation, or trend that makes this possible or timely now, but wasn't 2-3 years ago?"

*Why it matters:* Seeds the "Why Now" research dimension. If they can't answer, that dimension becomes more important to research autonomously.

---

**Q11 — Known risks**
> "What's the biggest risk you see with this idea right now?"

*Why it matters:* Whatever they say becomes the first assumption to test. Reveals self-awareness.
*Note:* Founders who say "none" are a bigger risk than the idea itself.

---

**Q12 — Known competitors**
> "Have you seen anything similar? Who would you consider a competitor, even indirectly?"

*Why it matters:* Starting points for Dimension 1 research. Always research beyond what they name.

---

## Tier 3: Deep Context (experienced or detailed founders)

**Q13 — Success definition**
> "What does success look like in 12 months? In 3 years?"

*Why it matters:* Reveals ambition level, shapes market sizing scope, surfaces VC-fundable vs lifestyle business framing.

---

**Q14 — Kill criteria**
> "What would make you abandon this idea?"

*Why it matters:* Pre-registers kill criteria from the founder's perspective. Useful reference for Stage 1-4 gate decisions.

---

**Q15 — Team gaps**
> "Who would you need on your team that you don't have today?"

*Why it matters:* Reveals feasibility gaps. Technical idea with no technical co-founder = flag.

---

## Follow-up probes (use when answers are vague)

| If they say... | Ask... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Lots of people have this problem" | "Can you name 3 specific people you've spoken to who have it?" |
| "There's no competition" | "What do people do today instead? Even imperfect solutions count." |
| "The market is huge" | "What's your source for that? How did you arrive at that number?" |
| "We'll figure out monetization later" | "Roughly — free, freemium, subscription, or something else?" |
| "Everyone will want this" | "Who is the one person who would pay for this tomorrow if you had it built?" |
| "It's AI-powered" | "What specifically does the AI do that couldn't be done without it?" |
